# Product Brief: avatar

## Executive Summary

`avatar` is a B2B white-label platform for agencies and studios that want to launch a recurring branded avatar content service for their clients. The strongest initial wedge is not selling "avatar content" as a standalone concept, but packaging the offer as an `always-on brand presence system` that helps brands maintain continuous social media presence.

The first target motion is agency-led, not direct-to-brand. Agencies use the platform as a branded service layer, then sell a recurring offer to end clients, especially in the `media / creators` segment.

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Agencies and studios struggle to deliver a continuous stream of branded social content efficiently. Manual production is slow, expensive, and difficult to scale across channels and markets.

### Problem Impact

- Agencies have limited production throughput and margin pressure.
- End clients need continuity, fast iteration, and channel adaptation.
- Existing delivery models often look like disconnected content packages rather than an ongoing system.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

- Traditional content production is labor-heavy and hard to standardize.
- Generic SaaS tools do not help agencies package the service under their own brand.
- Pure content subscriptions often fail to communicate durable strategic value.

### Proposed Solution

Provide a white-label agency platform that enables agencies to sell and deliver recurring branded avatar content as a systemized service. The offer combines:

- outcome: continuous brand presence on social media
- system: client portal, content calendar, branded workspace, and reusable avatar assets

### Key Differentiators

- White-label delivery for agencies instead of direct-to-brand only
- Hybrid `result + system` positioning
- Agency onboarding kit that reduces time-to-launch
- Clear path from add-on service to recurring offer

## Target Users

### Primary Users

- Agencies and studios
- Internal buyer / champion: `Product Lead`

### Secondary Users

- End-client brands served by the agency
- Strongest early end segment: `media / creators`
- Secondary future segments: `infobusiness / edtech`, `e-commerce brands`

### User Journey

1. An agency identifies an existing creative production client that needs more consistent branded social output.
2. The agency introduces the offer as a bundled enhancement inside current production services.
3. The agency delivers the service through a white-label workspace and client portal.
4. The client experiences recurring delivery through a content calendar, production visibility, and measurable output.
5. The agency validates the offer with a case study before broader rollout.

## Success Metrics

### Business Objectives

- Create a new productized service line for agencies
- Increase agency margin and recurring revenue potential
- Improve production throughput without proportional team growth

### Key Performance Indicators

- More content units produced within the same time window
- Faster delivery cadence for recurring social content
- Pilot-to-renewal or pilot-to-expansion rate
- Number of agencies able to launch using the onboarding kit

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

- White-label branded workspace for agencies
- White-label client portal
- Content calendar as the default operating screen
- Production status visibility
- Client request intake
- KPI / results visibility
- Avatar asset library
- Basic market localization support
- Deliverable templates for rapid service launch

### Out of Scope for MVP

- Broad multi-segment go-to-market beyond agencies first
- Full self-serve direct-to-brand model
- Deep enterprise customization
- Complex revenue-share-only pricing models
- Multi-channel rollout before the first single-channel pilot is validated

### MVP Success Criteria

- Agency can launch the offer as a bundled enhancement inside creative production
- Initial pilot on Instagram increases content output within the same time window
- Agency can package the offer clearly without it sounding like "just another content package"
- Pilot produces enough evidence to support a case study

### Future Vision

- Expand from bundled enhancement to a standalone pilot offer
- Turn the service into a recurring system sold across multiple channels
- Extend into additional end segments after agency proof is established
- Add stronger partner enablement: certification, co-selling, and packaged sales materials
